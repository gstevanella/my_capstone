#Sleeping Patterns
Did you know that social media stalking is a phenomenon that has been historically dismissed and only recently has started to receive more attention due to studies related to the potential correlation between compulsive use of social media and poor sleep patterns.
#Self-Disclosure & External Validation
Academic studies illustrate how the need for sharing, and sometimes oversharing, contents on social media is mainly related to two phenomena: online self-disclosure and stalking/information-seeking behaviours
#Lithium-ion_batteries
Did you know that the great majority of our mobiles relies on lithium-ion batteries? Lithium batteries are mainly made of lithium cobalt oxide and graphite (carbon). Some batteries use materials such as manganese instead; either way, the battery case is made of alluminium. Amnesty International invatigated human abuses in the Democratic Republic of Congo around the gobal trade in cobalt. Their findings are shocking and include child labour, fatal mines, unbearable working conditions, and long-term health damage. And just for $2 or $3 a day..
#New_Phone
Based on studies carried out by the World Bank, as the production of newer and more powerful devices is fastly increasing, the global metal consumption is growing at an yearly rate of 3% to 5%, meaning that, by 2050, our consumption of minerals will be higher than in the past 70,000 years.
#e-waste
Li-ion and Li-poly batteries (the ones in our phones, tablets and other small devices) are great to ensure product longevity, however, the incredibly high rate of disposal of the products that they power and lack of clear and harmonious regulation on how to manage e-waste translates into significant increase of environmental pollution along with consequences directly related to human health as a result of the toxicity of the materials that make the batteries.
Copper, cobalt, thallium, nickel, and silver, are to be considered among the main contributors in terms of both human toxicity and ecological impact.
#Ads Revenues
Some of the big tech companies generate billions of revenues from advertising, which are often successfully targeting our prefrences and leveraging our data.
#Datification
Did you know that some people are keen to track as much as possible about themselves via their digital devices such as phones, watches, home sensors, and laptops in order to find personal patterns and discover things they did not know about themselves?

Some of them would say that they are part of the 'quantified-self' movement, individuals who 'self-track' themselves. Although many devices do not even ask for permission to track our social media activities, they just do it and aggregate the results, sometimes selling them to third parties, others capitalising on the information for either making targeted recommendations or predicting consumers' behaviours.
#Online videos
In simple terms, videos 'travel' from the various data centres to our devices (smartphones, laptops, ipads, etc.) via different types of netowrk such as cables, modems, optical fiber, mobile networks; all these components require power, which can be granted through electricity, that typically creates a high consumption of resorces and a great production of C02 emissions.
Did tou know that, based on the Shift Project Research data, in 2018 online video viewing generated more than 300 metric tons of carbon dioxide? To get a better sense of quantities, just imagine that 10 hours of high-definition video encompasses more data than all English text on Wikipedia!
Data traffice is the culptrit for more thatn half of the digital tech global impact. Every byte stored or transferred needs large scale and powerful infrastructures, and, ad the end of the second decad of the 2000, videos represented 80% of global data traffic..and it does not seem on its way to decrease..
#Variable Reward System
There's a lot of talking about 'screen-time' and attention deficits, however, little has been done to stop the design of those online activities that leverage children's variable reward system, which is the exact same mechanism that lies behind gambling addictions. Youngsters'minds, who do not necessarily have a strong self-control yet, might become a fertile territory for developing obsessive behaviours.
